**Update: The Liberty Foundation has been wound down and is no longer actively fundraising. After the results of the 2016 election and the subsequent shift in corporate philanthropy, we were unable to secure enough funding to launch our initial Fellowship grants. Maybe we'll try again when the world is less awful!**

—

Through my time working on [https://xoxofest.com](XOXO) and [https://buildconf.com](Build), I've been lucky enough to meet a wealth of exceptionally talented independent artists and creators working across many different creative fields. However, a lot of these folks are struggling with the same problem: many are unable to break the cycle of having to burying their own creative interests beneath part-time or full-time work, living paycheck to paycheck without the necessary time and space to assess and develop their own sustainable independent creative practice. This project aims to help break that cycle.

**The _Liberty Foundation_ supports artists who work on the internet by giving them the tools they need to grow their creative practices into sustainable, independent businesses.**

The foundation will begin by offering a number of Fellowship awards. Under the guidance of our advisory board, we will be prioritizing awarding fellowships to a diverse and representative group of artists working in contemporary fields such as film, music, podcasts and audio storytelling, journalism, comics, tabletop and board games, and indie video games.

Our fellows will each receive a $60,000 cost of living grant, paid out monthly, and support for health insurance costs for one year. These grants will not be project-based, but rather aimed at giving creators a chance to step back and make decisions based on curiosity and sustainability rather than dire and immediate need.
 
The second aspect of the foundation supports this goal by providing an extensive coaching and mentor program to help artists maintain momentum, focus on their work, and grow it into a sustainable business. This will involve workshops and short courses focused on business development, marketing, financial planning, and legal support.
 
Our goal is to give as many artists and creators as possible the resources they need to build an independent career. With that in mind, we will also publish all the resources we produce for our fellows in a freely accessible online library for other creators to benefit from. Our ultimate goal is to remove as many barriers as possible for any artist who could be making a sustainable career out of their work.
 
Applications will open later this year. If you're interested in learning more, [follow us on Twitter](https://twitter.com/libertyfndn), or [sign up for our mailing list](http://eepurl.com/cOYW9n).

Thanks to our friends at [MailChimp](http://mailchimp.com) and our private benefactors for their support. If you'd like to help, or you're interested in becoming a benefactor, email [andy@theliberty.foundation](mailto:andy@theliberty.foundation).

— **Andy McMillan** · May 23, 2017
